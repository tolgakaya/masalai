# MasalAI — Marketing & Sales Plan
### 90-Day Launch Program + 12-Month Strategic Plan · Budget: ₺100,000

> Sixth document of the set (`docs/marketing-plan.md`). **This supersedes plan.md §7.4 (TR-first GTM)**: the strategy is now **English-first, global, multilingual** — every market served in its own language. plan.md §7.4 carries a supersession note.
>
> Written by the numbers a ₺100,000 (~$2,400 @ ₺42/$; re-check rate monthly) budget actually supports. An honest framing up front: **this budget buys learning and ignition, not scale.** Scale comes from three engines this plan builds: (1) the product's inherent virality (a child as the hero = the most shareable artifact on the internet), (2) creator gifting paid in product, not cash, (3) revenue reinvestment. Paid ads are a *measurement instrument* here, not a growth channel — yet.

## 1. Strategic Foundations

### 1.1 The three structural advantages (the whole plan leans on these)
1. **The output is the ad.** Every exported story video carries a watermark + end-card referral link (plan §11.3). A parent sharing their child's story to family WhatsApp/Instagram is doing our media buying for us. Marketing's #1 job is maximizing *shareable moments per user*, not impressions.
2. **Marginal localization cost ≈ 0.** LLM + TTS make each new language a config change plus voice QA (plan §2.3) — competitors localize with translators; we localize with a bake-off. "Her ülkeye kendi dilinde" is our defensible wedge: we can profitably serve locales too small for VC-funded competitors to bother with.
3. **Creators can be paid in product.** A personalized story of an influencer's own child costs us ~$1–2 COGS but carries ~$100–300 of gifting value and produces inherently emotional content. Our creator budget goes 10× further than cash-paying competitors'.

### 1.2 Positioning & message house (EN primary)
- **Category:** personalized bedtime storybooks (we say "storybook", never "AI content generator" — parents buy magic, not technology).
- **Core promise:** *"Your child, the hero of their own bedtime story — illustrated, narrated, in minutes."*
- **Three proof pillars** (every asset uses at least one):
  1. *Likeness:* "It actually looks like her." (the emotional kill-shot — always show the photo→character reveal)
  2. *Values:* "Stories that teach what you choose — sharing, courage, brushing teeth."
  3. *Trust:* "Photos deleted after character creation. Private by default." (privacy as a *selling* point, per ux-plan §11)
- **Tone:** warm, plain, parent-to-parent; zero AI-hype vocabulary. TR tagline stays "Bir varmış, bir yokmuş… bu sefer kahraman senin çocuğun."; EN: *"Once upon a time… it's your child."*

### 1.3 Market & language sequencing (12 months)
| Wave | When | Locales | Why this order |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Launch (M3) | **EN** (US/UK/CA/AU) + **TR** | EN = largest market + best creator ecosystem; TR = home-turf beachhead: cheapest CAC on earth for us, native community access, live funnel lab for every experiment before it spends EN budget |
| 2 | Months 4–6 | **DE, ES** | DE: purchasing power + huge TR diaspora (warm entry); ES: 500M speakers, strong family-gifting culture, cheap CPMs |
| 3 | Months 7–9 | **AR (Gulf), PT-BR, FR** | Gulf: highest gifting spend/capita + underserved in personalized kids content; PT-BR: viral short-video culture |
| 4 | Months 10–12 | NL, IT, PL + on-demand | Data-driven: any locale where organic signups cluster gets a language within 4 weeks (our structural advantage as policy) |
Localization DoD per locale: UI strings + 2 QA'd TTS voices + 10 template/SEO pages + 5 localized creator posts + local gift-occasion calendar. Owner: you + Claude (content), 1 native-speaker freelance QA pass (~₺1.500/locale from the budget).

### 1.4 Timeline reconciliation with the dev plan (Claude Code sprint mode)
Dev plan §17 (sprint edition): **soft launch Day 12–14**. Marketing re-phases accordingly:
- **Founding Families pre-sale** still opens in week 1 (Day 2–3): landing + MoR payment link. Same offer ($14.99 → 3 credits + lifetime 20% + credits-page name), cap 200 — but now it converts to live product access within ~10 days, which makes it an easier sell ("your access starts next week", not "someday").
- **Concierge selling is demoted** from revenue pillar to an optional Etsy side-channel (the platform arrives too fast to need manual fulfillment as a bridge). Etsy stays in the plan as a US-demand test lab and gift-card channel from week 3.
- **The launch calendar inverts the classic mistake:** Day 12–14 is a quiet *soft* launch (waitlist + creators + referral loop). The loud moment — Product Hunt + newsletter burst + coordinated creator wave — fires **week 3–4 on a stability-proven product**. You only get one PH day; we don't spend it on day-2-of-production.

## 3. Funnel Math — honest targets (base / stretch), sprint edition
Assumptions from plan §7.4 KPIs: visitor→free story 8–12%, free→paid 10–15%, viral: each completed story ≈ 0.9 shares, each share ≈ 1.5 visits. Product is live from ~Day 14.

| Metric (cumulative unless noted) | Day 30 | Day 60 | Day 90 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Product status | live ~2 wks (soft) | live 6 wks, PH done | live 10 wks, 1st locale wave prepping |
| Founding pre-sales (× $14.99, cap 200) | 120 / 200 | 200 (cap) | — |
| Free stories generated | 150 / 350 | 700 / 1,600 | 1,600 / 3,500 |
| Paying customers (platform) | 35 / 90 | 120 / 280 | 260 / 560 |
| **Monthly revenue run-rate** | $700 / $1,800 | $1,900 / $4,400 | **$3,400 / $7,800 (₺143k / ₺328k)** |

**What "ciddi potansiyel" must mean at day 90** (the go/scale gate, unchanged and still more important than the revenue line): (a) blended CAC < $5 with one channel at <$3, (b) viral coefficient K ≥ 0.25 measured, (c) free→paid ≥ 10%, (d) ≥ 25% of buyers outside TR, (e) week-4 repeat/gift purchase ≥ 15%. Hit 4 of 5 → Q2 scales with reinvested revenue; miss → Q2 iterates positioning/creative, and the plan says so out loud instead of pretending. Note the sprint's honest trade-off: earlier revenue, but the audience-building runway before launch shrinks from 9 weeks to 1 — weeks 3–6 therefore carry the audience work the old Phase A did.

## 4. 90-Day Program — sprint edition (synchronized with plan §17)

### PHASE A — Sprint week (Days 1–7 · dev: S0→S2)
- **Day 1–2:** brand kit applied, EN+TR landing with waitlist live, PostHog + UTM discipline on, social handles created (EN + TR tracks), MoR account applied (blocking item — Day 1, hour 1).
- **Day 2–3:** **Founding Families pre-sale opens** (landing + MoR payment link). Announce: personal networks, LinkedIn, 3 TR parenting FB groups, 2 EN subreddit value-threads. Founder-led DMs start (10/day, §7).
- **Day 3–7:** content engine warm-up — 1 short/day per language track from the sprint itself (the progress screen, the character-sheet reveal: build-in-public is the cheapest pre-launch content there is); creator list to 150 names; first 20 gifting DMs (offer: beta access Day 8 + founding perks); 2 newsletter micro-sponsorships booked for week 3–4 (the PH window, not launch day).

### PHASE B — Beta + soft launch (Days 8–14 · dev: beta blitz → launch)
- **Day 8–9:** founding beta blitz — buyers + 8 creators generate real stories; collect UGC with signed usage rights; 3 remote moderated UX sessions (ux-plan §13, compressed).
- **Day 10–11:** creators' reveal posts go live (their genuine beta content = launch-week ammunition); e-mail #2 to waitlist ("doors open this week").
- **Day 12–14: SOFT LAUNCH.** Waitlist e-mail, invite flag off, referral loop on ("gift a free story"), paid tests begin small (TikTok $12/day + Meta $8/day, 6 creatives from beta UGC, single KPI: cost per free-story-started).

### PHASE C — Loud launch + measured ignition (Weeks 3–13)
- **W3–4:** **Product Hunt launch** + newsletter sponsorships + coordinated creator wave (15 more gifted) — the concentrated burst, now on a stable product with real testimonials. SEO template pages 15→30.
- **W5–8:** creative kill/scale cycle weekly (kill >$2.5/free-story after 2k impressions; +50% on <$1.5); second creator wave targeted by W3 conversion data; Etsy gift-card channel live; DE/ES localization production starts (strings + voice QA) so month 4 opens localized.
- **W9–13:** double down reserve line only if CAC gate met; "1,000 stories" milestone content; birthday-automation e-mails start compounding; **Day-90 gate review (§3) writes the Q2 plan.**

**Weekly operating rhythm (founder, ~10 h/wk on marketing — sprint week realistically 5 h, protected):** Mon metrics + budget (30 min, DECISIONS.md note) · Tue–Thu content batch + community (45 min/day) · Fri creator outreach block (10 DMs) + next-week calendar.

## 6. Channel Playbook (priority order — ranked by fit-to-budget, not fashion)
1. **Short-form organic (TikTok/Reels/Shorts)** — the engine. 4 repeatable formats: (a) photo→character reveal, (b) child's live reaction watching their story (creators), (c) "which moral does your kid need?" hooks, (d) behind-the-magic (progress screen). 1/day/language-track; winners get remade, not mourned.
2. **Creator gifting at scale** — §1.1-3. 150-name list, 10 DMs/week forever; the deal: free story + founding perks for a post with usage rights. Paid micro only after their organic post proves resonance.
3. **Etsy / gift marketplaces** — rented demand for concierge + post-launch gift cards. Margin after fees ~55–65%; also our cheapest US-market test lab.
4. **Referral + watermark loop** — product-native (plan §12.4); marketing's job is making sharing celebratory (end-card copy: "Made for Defne with ❤️ — make one for your hero").
5. **Programmatic SEO** — compounding asset (plan §12.3); 15 pages/locale at launch, +10/month; each with playable sample = dwell-time moat.
6. **Newsletter micro-sponsorships** — precise, cheap, launch-burst amplifier.
7. **Paid social** — measurement instrument in Q1, scaling channel from Q2 only with reinvested revenue and only into proven creative.
8. **Explicitly NOT doing (year 1):** Google Search ads (intent exists but CPCs eat this budget), influencer cash deals >₺5k, brand campaigns, events, PR agencies, TV — written down so future-you doesn't relitigate under pressure.

## 7. Sales Motions (because "pazarlama" alone doesn't hit month-1 revenue)
- **Founder-led DM sales (W2→):** every waitlist signup with a public profile gets a personal thank-you + founding offer. Target 10 conversations/day; a 5% close = the month-1 number. Script in 3 languages, kept human.
- **Concierge → platform migration (W9):** every concierge buyer gets launch credits equal to their spend — Etsy customers become platform accounts (list-building with revenue attached).
- **Gift-occasion calendar selling:** birthdays are the evergreen (collected at character creation, plan §12.2 automation); seasonal peaks per locale mapped (Christmas, Yılbaşı, Eid al-Fitr ~Mar '27, 23 Nisan, Mother's/Father's Days per country) with campaigns briefed 4 weeks ahead.
- **B2B seedlings (Q3):** kindergarten pilot sold as "classroom moral of the month" — 5 pilots free, case-studied, priced per-classroom in Q4.

## 8. Brand-safety & platform-risk rules (non-negotiable, mirrors plan §5)
Ads/content NEVER use real children's faces without written parental consent (creators sign usage agreements covering their own kids); no before/after child imagery in paid ads (platform policy trap); no "AI" claims that trigger synthetic-media ad policies — always disclose personalization mechanics honestly in landing copy; COPPA-safe ad targeting (parents, never children); every locale's ad claims reviewed against local consumer law before spend (EN/DE especially).

## 9. Measurement stack & review cadence
PostHog (product) + UTM sheet + Stripe/Etsy revenue + a single **North-Star dashboard: completed stories/week** (correlates with everything). Weekly: CAC by channel, K-factor, free→paid, revenue vs §3 table. Monthly: cohort retention, locale P&L (even tiny), budget line burn vs plan. Everything reviewed in the Monday 30-min ritual; every pivot logged in DECISIONS.md — marketing runs on the same protocol discipline as engineering.

## 10. Top risks (ranked) & pre-decided responses
1. **Organic doesn't catch (most likely):** →W7 gate: if creator beta posts underperform (<2% engagement), the problem is the *reveal moment*, not distribution — fix the demo asset before spending W9 ads.
2. **Ad accounts flag child-adjacent content:** → §8 rules + backup: shift budget to creators/newsletters (accounts can't ban gifting).
3. **Sprint slips past Day 14:** → plan §17 slip rule: launch moves to Day 21, beta window widens, PH stays in week 4–5. Phases are pegged to dev gates, not calendar dates; founding cap raises to 300 to bridge. Never launch untested to save a date.
4. **TRY volatility eats the budget:** → convert 60% of budget to USD-denominated spend commitments in month 1.
5. **A funded competitor copies the wedge:** → speed on locale coverage + trust positioning (our privacy UX is expensive to fake) + community depth.
